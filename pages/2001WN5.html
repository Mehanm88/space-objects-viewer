2001 WN5

Asteroid (153814) 2001 WN5 is a large, stony asteroid classified as potentially hazardous. Measuring around 935 meters (3,068 feet) in diameter, it orbits the Sun every 1.72 years. 2001 WN5 made a notable close approach to Earth on June 26, 2028, when it will pass within 248,000 kilometers (154,000 miles)—closer than the Moon. This close encounter will provide astronomers with a unique opportunity to study the asteroid in detail using radar and optical telescopes. Due to its size and proximity, 2001 WN5 is classified as potentially hazardous, though there is no risk of impact during its 2028 approach. Scientists are particularly interested in large objects like 2001 WN5, as they are capable of causing widespread devastation if they were to collide with Earth.
